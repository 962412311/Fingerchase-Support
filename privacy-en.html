Fingerchase Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 18, 2026

1. Scope and Provider

This policy applies to the Fingerchase app and its official support pages. “We” means Parsifal Chen, the app provider shown on the App Store. Apple, GitHub, websites opened from the app, and destinations you choose in the system share sheet process information under their own terms and privacy policies.

2. Privacy Summary

Fingerchase is designed to work primarily on your device. In the current release, we do not operate a server that receives or stores your practice history, personal scores, imported images, PDFs, audio files, microphone audio, or recognition results. Network activity may still occur when you use Apple subscription services, open an external link, visit these support pages, submit a GitHub support request, or share text through another app.

3. Information Stored on Your Device

The app may store practice and viewing history, favorites, settings, onboarding and daily-goal choices, trial and entitlement counters, permanently unlocked pieces, personal-score metadata and files, conversion results, piano-calibration data, and recognition-optimization parameters. This information is used only to provide the feature you requested, restore local state, personalize practice, and enforce free-trial or subscription access. It is not sent to us through an app-operated server.

4. Files and User-Provided Content

Structured scores, images, PDFs, and audio are accessed only after you explicitly select them with the system file picker. The app may copy the selected file into its private app storage, create temporary working files, and save derived score or conversion results. Personal content is not uploaded to us. The app does not provide public links, export, printing, or sharing for personal scores, note data, source files, or recordings. You should import only content you have the right to use and avoid importing information you consider highly sensitive.

5. Microphone Processing

Microphone permission is requested only when you start real-piano practice, calibration, or recognition optimization. Audio frames are analyzed on the device while the feature is active; raw microphone audio is not uploaded to us or saved by the app as a recording. Derived calibration or optimization parameters may remain on the device until you delete them. Because an active microphone can capture nearby sounds, use the feature only when appropriate and stop it before private conversations.

6. Subscriptions, Purchases, and Diagnostics

Apple processes purchases, renewals, refunds, payment credentials, fraud prevention, and Apple Account information. The app receives limited StoreKit information such as product, transaction verification, subscription state, and expiration so it can provide Pro access; we do not receive your full card details. Depending on your device and Apple privacy settings, Apple may also make limited crash reports or aggregated diagnostics available to help us fix defects. Apple controls its own collection, security, and retention.

7. Information We Do Not Collect

The current release does not require a Fingerchase account and does not request your name, email address, contacts, precise location, health data, biometric data, or unrestricted access to your photo or file library. We do not include third-party advertising or analytics SDKs, use an advertising identifier, track you across other companies' apps or websites, create advertising profiles, sell personal information, or use personal data for decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects.

8. Third Parties, External Links, and Sharing

Apple provides App Store and subscription services. These support pages are hosted by GitHub Pages; GitHub states that it logs visitors' IP addresses for security purposes. A GitHub Issue is public and may require a GitHub account, so never include passwords, payment information, personal scores, recordings, or other sensitive information. Links to score sources or other websites send your browser request, including information such as your IP address, to that site. When you deliberately use the system share sheet for a practice-summary text or invitation text, the destination you select receives that text under its own privacy practices. Fingerchase does not automatically send personal score content to these parties.

9. Purposes and Legal Basis

Where applicable law requires a legal basis, local processing is performed to provide features you request and fulfill the app service, based on your choices and any necessary permission; subscription processing is necessary to provide a purchase you request; limited diagnostics may be used for the legitimate interest of maintaining security and reliability; and information may be handled when required by law. You can withdraw microphone permission or stop using optional file, link, support, and sharing features at any time. Withdrawal does not affect processing already completed before withdrawal.

10. Retention, Deletion, and Backups

Personal scores and their related practice data, calibration data, and recognition-optimization data can be deleted in the app. Other local history, favorites, settings, and access state remain until the app's data is removed or the app is uninstalled. Uninstalling removes the app's active local container from that device, but copies may remain in device or cloud backups according to your Apple backup settings and Apple's retention rules, and may reappear if a backup is restored. Subscription records are retained by Apple and deleting the app does not cancel a subscription. GitHub and external sites retain their own logs or submitted content under their policies; public support issues may remain visible until edited or deleted through GitHub.

11. Your Choices and Rights

You can revoke microphone permission in system settings, delete supported local content in the app, uninstall the app to remove its remaining active local data, manage or delete relevant backups through Apple, manage subscriptions in your Apple Account, and choose not to open external links or use sharing. Because we do not hold an app-operated server copy of your local content, we normally cannot access, export, correct, or delete that content for you. Depending on your location, you may have rights to information, access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection, portability, or withdrawal of consent for personal data actually held by a controller. Requests concerning Apple, GitHub, or another destination must be directed to that provider.

12. Security and Residual Risks

We reduce risk by using app-scoped storage, system permission controls, explicit file selection, and on-device processing. No device, software, backup, or transmission method is completely secure. Residual risks include loss or unauthorized access to an unlocked, shared, compromised, or backed-up device; exposure through a destination you select; public disclosure in a GitHub Issue; operating-system or provider incidents; malicious or malformed imported files; and corruption or accidental loss of local data. Use a device passcode, keep the operating system updated, review backup and sharing settings, keep independent lawful copies of important files, and do not submit sensitive content through public support channels.

13. Children's Privacy

Fingerchase does not require an account, provide targeted advertising, or knowingly collect children's personal information through an app-operated server. A parent or guardian should supervise a minor's device use, file imports, microphone use, external links, sharing, and purchases as required by local law. Apple provides the applicable purchase and family controls.

14. International Processing

We do not operate an app-content server or transfer your personal scores, practice history, or microphone audio internationally. Apple, GitHub, external websites, and share destinations may process information in other countries or regions under their own privacy notices and legal safeguards.

15. Changes and Contact

We will update this policy and its effective date before or when a material change to our data practices is introduced. For general privacy questions, use our public support page. Because GitHub Issues are public, describe the question without personal or sensitive information. Local app data should be managed using the controls described above; Apple- or GitHub-held data must be handled through those providers.
