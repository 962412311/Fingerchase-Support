User Privacy Choices

For Fingerchase

1. What You Control Locally

Fingerchase does not keep an app-operated server copy of your practice history, personal scores, imported files, microphone audio, or conversion results. Most privacy choices therefore take effect on your device rather than through an online account request.

2. Manage System Permissions

You can disable Fingerchase microphone access at any time in the system settings on your iPhone, iPad, or Mac. Images, PDFs, audio, and structured scores are processed only after you explicitly select them with the system file picker. The app does not continuously access your file library, so there is no separate file-library permission to disable.

3. Delete On-Device Content

You can delete personal scores and their related practice data, piano calibration data, and recognition-optimization data in the app. Other local history, favorites, and settings are removed with the app's data when you uninstall it. Confirm that you no longer need the content before uninstalling.

4. Review Device Backups

Uninstalling removes active app data from that device, but an existing device or cloud backup may retain a copy and may restore it later. Review or delete relevant backups through your Apple device or account settings. Fingerchase cannot inspect or delete Apple-held backups for you.

5. Manage Your Subscription

Apple manages subscriptions, renewals, cancellations, and refunds. You can view or cancel Fingerchase Pro in your Apple Account subscription settings. Deleting the app does not automatically cancel a subscription.

6. Accounts, Tracking, and Advertising

Fingerchase currently does not require an account, provide targeted advertising, or sell personal information. There are therefore no separate account profiling, advertising tracking, or data sale options to disable.

7. External Links, Support, and Sharing

You may choose not to open external source links or use the system share sheet. These support pages are hosted by GitHub Pages, which logs visitor IP addresses for security. GitHub Issues are public. Never submit passwords, payment information, personal scores, recordings, or other sensitive information there. Manage Apple- or GitHub-held information directly with that provider.

8. Limits and Data-Loss Risk

Because local data is not copied to a Fingerchase server, we normally cannot recover it after deletion, device loss, corruption, or an unavailable backup, and cannot remotely access, export, correct, or delete it for you. Keep independent lawful copies of important source files and protect your device with a passcode and current software.

9. Contact Us

If you cannot complete a privacy choice using the options above, use our public support page and describe the issue without personal or sensitive information. Because we do not hold a server copy of local app content, deletion of that content must be completed on the device or through the relevant backup provider.
